GST 2.0 Overhaul

Effective September 22, 2025 • Source: 56th GST Council

Simpler slabs, lower compliance, demand boost, and key fixes for inverted duties and insurance taxation.

New Slab Structure

🥗

5% Merit

Essentials & common-use items

⚙️

18% Standard

Default for most goods & services

🚫💎

40% Sin & Luxury

Demerit & luxury items

What Gets Cheaper

To 5% (from 12%/18%)

  • ✅ Packaged foods: sauces, processed essentials
  • ✅ Dairy: butter, cheese, other dairy items
  • ✅ Household: soaps, toothpaste, hair oil, toiletries
  • ✅ Apparel & footwear: common textiles and footwear

To 18% (from 28%)

  • ✅ Automobiles: small cars and two-wheelers
  • ✅ Consumer durables: TVs, refrigerators, ACs, washing machines

What Gets Costlier

🚬

To 40%

Tobacco & products, pan masala

🥤🚗

To 40%

Aerated beverages, luxury automobiles

Key Policy Reforms

🛡️

Insurance Exempt

Individual life and health insurance fully exempt from GST.

🧮

Inverted Duty Fix

Corrected in textiles, fertilizers; reduces ITC accumulation.

⚡

Ease of Compliance

Faster refunds and simplified registration, esp. for MSMEs.

Business Action Checklist

Rate mapping

Reclassify SKUs/HSNs/SACs to 5%/18%/40%.

Systems update

ERP/POS/invoicing, e-invoice & e-way bill codes.

Pricing

Revise MRPs/margins; assess anti-profiteering risk.

Contracts

Trigger GST change clauses; update schedules.

Inventory

Relabel, credit notes for pre-change stock.

ITC planning

Model inverted duty unwind & utilization.

Cash flow

Reforecast working capital; refund timelines.

Compliance

Returns, LUTs/bonds, TCS/TDS alignment.

Communication

Notify channels; update website & catalogues.

Anticipated Economic Impact

🛒

Demand Boost

Lower prices raise purchasing power.

📉

Lower Inflation

Relief on daily essentials.

🏭

Competitiveness

Simpler structure lowers compliance costs.

Open Points to Monitor

  • Item-level lists for 5% / 18% / 40% and luxury thresholds.
  • HSN/SAC schedules, exemptions, and any cess interactions.
  • Transitional rules for MRPs, debit/credit notes, ongoing supplies.
  • Approach to anti-profiteering under the new structure.
